Plan S

  • OA Initiative launched by "cOAlition S"
  • Consortium of institutions from at least 12 different European countries
  • Main goal: open access for state-funded research
  • Supported by the European Commission
  • Stipulate 10 principles

Plan S

  • Stipulate 10 principles
    1. Authors retain copyright and publish OA
    2. Create robust requirements for eligible journals
    3. Provide incentives for creation of eligible journals
    4. Publication fees should be covered by institutions
    5. Publication fees should be standardized & capped

Plan S

  • Stipulate 10 principles
    1. Institutions should align their OA policies
    2. OA books are tough and will require more time
    3. Open repositories are vital
    4. Hybrid OA is not compliant with key principle
    5. Non-compliance is monitored & sanctioned

Open Access in Austria

Open Access in Austria

  • Big proponent of open access
  • National Open Access Strategy adopted in 2016
  • Open Science Policy in 2022
    • Make all publicly funded research OA by 2025
    • Integration with EOSC
    • Strong focus on both open access and open data
    • Creation of Austrian open science infrastructure

Open Access in Austria

  • Austrian Science Fund (FWF)
    • State-sponsored research funder
    • Most important funder in Austria
    • Early member of cOAlition S
    • Mandatory open access since 2015
    • Funds associated open access costs

Open Access in Austria

  • Austrian Science Fund (FWF)
    • Requires immediate open access
      • Gold OA
      • Green OA
      • Hybrid OA
    • Requires CC BY license or equivalent
    • Hybrid OA requires transformative agreements
    • Hybrid OA is impermissible starting 2027

Open Access in Austria

  • Konsortien E-Medien Österreich (KEMÖ)
    • Nation-wide consortium for negotiating agreements with academic publishers
    • Transformative agreements with major publishers
    • Transformative agreements with minor publishers
    • Most universities are members

Open Access in Austria

  • Legal right to self-archive
    • Law was introduced in 2015
    • Right to publish accepted manuscript
    • After 12 months embargo
    • For scientific publications
    • From institutions with 50%+ public funding
    • In journals with more than one issue per year

Open Access in Germany

  • Big proponent of open access
  • No uniform national open access policy
  • Coalition Agreement 2021-2025: strengthen OA
  • Bottom-Up Approach
    • Individual states have OA policies

Open Access in Germany

  • Deutsche Forschungsgemeinschaft (DFG)
    • State-sponsored research funder
    • Most important funder in Germany
    • Not a member of cOAlition S
    • Only recommends open access
    • Subsidies for associated open access costs

Open Access in Germany
